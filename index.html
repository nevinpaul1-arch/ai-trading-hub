What is AI Trading — and can it grow your money?

AI trading means using algorithms, machine learning, and data science to make investment decisions faster, more consistently, and with less emotion than a human trader. This hub teaches you everything from the ground up — with verified information and honest expectations.

Brief history
1970s–80s
Program trading emerges. Rule-based portfolio insurance systems start executing trades automatically.
1988
Renaissance Technologies launches Medallion Fund using statistical pattern-recognition — no human intuition, only math.
2000s
High-frequency trading explodes. Co-location in exchange data centers, microsecond execution, HFT accounts for majority of US equity volume.
2013–2018
Deep learning (LSTMs, CNNs) applied to price prediction. Two Sigma, D.E. Shaw hire physicists and ML researchers en masse.
2020–2023
Transformer models and NLP transform how machines read earnings calls, news, and SEC filings — in milliseconds.
2025–2026
Agentic AI systems plan, research, and execute multi-step trading workflows with minimal human oversight. 47% of major hedge funds have GenAI in production. (Source: Hedgeweek, 2026)

The firms that proved it works
🏅
Renaissance Technologies — Medallion Fund
Since 1988, the Medallion Fund has reportedly returned ~66% gross per year (~39% net after fees). A $100 investment in 1988 would have grown to roughly $398 million by 2018. The fund has been closed to outside investors since 1993. No retail investor can access it. Its methods are not publicly disclosed, but are believed to involve statistical arbitrage, pattern recognition, and multiple ML models across asset classes. Source: Multiple financial publications; Wikipedia
Σ
Two Sigma
Uses ML across satellite imagery, credit card data, social sentiment, and SEC filings. Two Sigma's own researchers publicly warn: "AI will not magically solve key challenges like predicting the future from past data and avoiding overfitting." Honest from the top. Source: Two Sigma 2026 Outlook
C
Citadel Securities
Dominant market maker. Uses AI for HFT — executing thousands of quotes per second. Returned 38% in 2022 when most funds lost money. HFT requires billions in infrastructure; not a retail strategy. Source: Public reporting
MG
Man Group — AHL & AlphaGPT
Man Group's quant equity unit built an internal tool called AlphaGPT — an AI that proposes signals, writes backtesting code, runs the backtest, and sends output to human researchers for review. This is a real, confirmed workflow. Source: Bloomberg, 2026

The forex market trades approximately $7.5 trillion per day — the largest financial market on Earth. It runs 24 hours a day, 5 days a week across major global sessions. AI is well-suited here because currency prices are driven by measurable macroeconomic data: interest rates, inflation, employment, and GDP.

AI in Crypto Trading

Crypto is arguably the most AI-compatible market: it never closes, generates unique on-chain data not available in traditional markets, and its volatility creates pricing inefficiencies. An estimated 65% of all cryptocurrency trading volume involves some form of automation.
